What is Light Communication (LC)?

Light Communication (LC), also known as Li-Fi (Light Fidelity), is a wireless communication technology that uses visible light, infrared, or ultraviolet light to transmit data. Unlike traditional Wi-Fi, which relies on radio waves, LC leverages the rapid modulation of light sourcesâ€”such as LEDsâ€”to send information at incredibly high speeds.

This technology enables secure, high-speed, and interference-free data transmission, making it ideal for environments where radio frequency communication is not feasible or safe, such as hospitals, airplanes, and industrial facilities.

Light communication, specifically Visible Light Communication (VLC) and Li-Fi, is a wireless data transmission technology that uses light instead of radio waves to transmit information. VLC uses the visible light spectrum (400 to 800 THz) to send data by modulating the intensity of light from a light source. Li-Fi, a form of VLC, uses LEDs to transmit data, offering potential for high-speed wireless networks.

Why Light Communication (LC)?

The available radio frequency spectrum is not enough to cater to the growing demand for wireless data transmission. The number of devices accessing mobile networks is increasing every second, resulting in inefficiency, congestion, and high interference.

This has led to the birth of Light Communication, which unlocks unparalleled data and bandwidth at higher speedsâ€”enabling a seamless and more efficient user experience. By leveraging the untapped spectrum of visible light, LC provides a promising solution for the future of high-speed, secure, and interference-free communication.
